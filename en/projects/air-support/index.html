Air Support

06/19/2021 3-minute read

Air Support is a digital assistant for optimal control of indoor air quality, which I am implementing as a product of my master’s thesis.

Motivation

Air quality is a fundamental factor influencing human beings. Under poor climatic conditions, performance suffers. With increased pollutant concentrations of the air health damages threaten. Consequently, it is of relevance to ensure the best possible air quality in the environment of people.

Inadequately ventilated indoor areas reach air pollution levels over a hundred times higher than outdoor areas. To make matters worse, Central Europeans spend an average of 90% of their time indoors. The currently prevailing corona pandemic is making the issue of indoor air quality more important than ever before. Frequent ventilation becomes an important measure in the fight against the virus. Apart from the impact on humans, intelligent ventilation is crucial for resource-efficient heating and building maintenance. Despite the relevance of fresh air supply, the vast majority of buildings are ventilated manually. This is prone to errors. Because of hoped-for financial savings or lack of awareness of the current indoor climate, ventilation is careless and infrequent. Furthermore, efficient room ventilation is complex. Arbitrary ventilation may not lead to the desired effect of improved indoor climate. Reasons can be wrong ventilation, worse conditions of outdoor air or thermodynamic effects of the building.

Previous explanations explain the need to make manual window ventilation intelligent. As part of my master’s thesis, I set “Air Support” as a goal to develop a digital assistant that supports people in creating a healthy indoor climate by providing intelligent instructions for manual window ventilation. The system claims to take into account historical, current and future influencing parameters from sources both internal and external to the room. The system’s real-time relevant instructions inform the user about the optimal way and time of the next ventilation procedure.

Collaboration with artiso solutions GmbH

I am implementing Air Support and the associated master thesis together with artiso solutions GmbH, a software services company. The office of artiso solutions GmbH serves as a test environment for Air Support.

Test setup

An IoT infrastructure is set up in the office of artiso solutions GmbH. Rooms of different key data are equipped with sensors to collect data of relevant parameters. Outdoor air quality is collected via third-party APIs and sensor technology in the outdoor area of the office. Measured values are collected in cycles of a few minutes. Window opening is detected ad-hoc via window contacts.

Employees work and interact with the spaces as usual. They produce carbon dioxide by breathing, opening doors and windows. A Microsoft Teams Channel is used for each room, which receives prompts for ventilation via incoming webhooks when indoor air quality is poor. The IoT infrastructure is the prerequisite for generating the data that is important for the following modeling.
